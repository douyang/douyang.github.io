About

I am a cardiologist and researcher in the Division of Research at Kaiser Permanente Northern California and an adjunct Assistant Professor at the Smidt Heart Institute at Cedars-Sinai. I majored in statistics at Rice University, obtained my MD at UCSF, and received post-graduate medical education in internal medicine, cardiology, and a postdoc in computer science and biomedical data science at Stanford University. I am also a Deputy Editor at NEJM AI and Chair of the ImageGuideRegistry for the American Society of Echocardiography.

Research

Our mission is to improve cardiovascular diagnosis and disease detection using computer vision and artificial intelligence. We focus on three pillars: AI model development (precision phenotyping), AI model understanding (interpretable medical AI), and AI model deployment (clinical trials). Our work has been published in Nature, Nature Medicine, NEJM AI, Circulation, JAMA Cardiology, and the European Heart Journal.

The best part of the job are the people I get to work with. I have the good fortune of working with a great group of motivated, talented, and creative individuals from a wide range of backgrounds. Our lab members bring expertise from a wide range of backgrounds ranging from physics, mechanical engineering, data science, math, and computer science to cardiology, anesthesia, and internal medicine.
